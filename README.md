## About

I build end-to-end AI systems — the retrieval layer, the orchestration logic, the API surface, and the interface people actually use.

My background is in frontend engineering, which shaped how I think about AI products: latency, failure states, and interaction design matter as much as model choice. I moved into AI engineering because the interesting problems sit at the seams — between language models and the systems that have to make them reliable, observable, and cost-effective.

Most of my work involves grounding models in real data, giving them tools, and keeping them accountable to business constraints. Less prompt, more architecture.
